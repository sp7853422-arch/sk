Top 5 Influential Features from SHAP Analysis
This summary highlights the five most impactful features identified using SHAP global interpretability for the student pass/fail prediction model. Each feature is explained in terms of its relationship with the prediction target.
1. Study Time
Definition: Number of hours a student dedicates to studying per week.

Impact: Higher study time strongly increases the likelihood of passing.

2. Absences
Definition: Total number of school days missed.

Impact: More absences significantly reduce the probability of passing.

3. Parental Education
Definition: Highest education level attained by the student's parents.

Impact: Higher parental education correlates with better academic outcomes and increased pass probability.
4. Test Score
Definition: Final exam or assessment score.

Impact: Higher scores directly boost the chance of passing.

5. Internet Access
Definition: Whether the student has reliable internet access at home.

Impact: Lack of internet access is associated with lower pass rates due to limited learning resources.
